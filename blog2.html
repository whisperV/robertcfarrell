Good news is I found a cool new browser - Vivaldi, plus cryptocurrency seems to be really taking off across multiple industries. I've been swimming in a project related to crypto which is pretty ambitious, but I hope it will shed some light... somewhere in the vast wilderness that is crypto. Crypto is a liability minefield in many ways, but the technology has the potential to change the world. Imagine being able to create a general ledger for all of a company's transactions completely error free. Not to mention it's a system specifically designed using some of the best available cryptography protocols to ensure the data is tamper free. The ledger is automatically amended for every transaction, it's error free and very nearly tamper-proof.

It's a dangerous field to be regardless of potential liabilities,partly because it promises to disrupt so many industries. There are elements across multiple industries that would like to to pull crypto into the current financial regulatory schemes in existence. The problem there is crypto is like nothing we've seen before. Any attempt to cram crypto into current rules will expand the rules of the current regulatory system to the point authorities will be able to pull the trigger based on a whim and not sound legal, accounting or financial principles. Calls for mark to market treatment of crypto would chill trading, and especially mining. The crypto miners are the backbone of the blockchain and forcing IPOs instead of ICOs would only serve to hobble innovation in the countries applying that force.

After witnessing Elon Musk, a private citizen, launch his personal car into space it's very difficult to get behind widespread regulation of crypto. Currently the underlying technology is solving many problems regulations sought to solve in a prior age. Many of the rules and regulations relating to finance breakdown at some point when applied to crypto, and the breakdowns occur at different points for different coins or tokens or smart contract. An entirely new regulatory regime would have to be drafted and it would have to span multiple disciplines. Unfortunately, the current system cannot keep up with the technology and regulating it using, in this context, antiquated rules will only serve to disable the industry. This desire to stifle crypto is strong among many of those who depend on the current rules and do not have the wherewithal to adapt. The benefits to everyone if crypto succeeds far outweigh the personal benefits the "poo pooers," for lack of a better term, and I feel it's important more people see that.

The Big Problem of the Day

Today's big problem is how to handle a changed mind. Or more accuratley how the caculator handles a changed mind. If contributions were already made to an IRA, the contributed amounts reduce the controbution limit. As of this writing, the calculator will take an amount entered in field appearing if the user checks Yes when asked about already made IRA contributions. The limit will be reduced appropriately, however if No is later checked, the reduction will remain. I haven't implemented a solution yet as there are two approaches I could take to deal with this. The first is to include logic which will recalculate the limit without prior contributions, or disable all inputs after the calculator is run. There are pros and cons to each, although the preferable solution would be to allow a user to change their answers on the fly and still get an accurate answer. This is a bit of a challenege, partly due to my inexperience but also because it involves tracing the previously entered contribution amounts throughout the program. I spent some time trying to get the contributed amount zeroed out, however it is proving a bit more challenging than expected. I will revisit this next time I work on this project and mull it over in the meantime.
